About Me

I am a 3rd-year Engineering Science student at the University of Toronto, majoring in Machine Intelligence, with a strong interest in differential privacy, cybersecurity, and software development. Currently, I am pursuing an internship at Vector Institute, supervised by Prof. Nicolas Papernot. Growing up, I traveled extensively due to my dad's profession as a Ship Captain—I am Singaporean and have lived in Montreal, Dubai, Kolkata, and Toronto. These experiences have shaped me into an adaptable and globally-minded individual. In my spare time, I enjoy visual arts, yoga, and reading.

Forged Training Distributions in Differential Privacy

  • Research Intern at the Vector Institute for AI with CleverHans Lab (focus on ML Privacy, security and fairness) starting summer 2024.
  • Implementing DP-SGD algorithms to investigate the uniqueness of machine learning training distributions.
  • Currently working towards a paper on forged training distributions, and its real-world implications, such as copyright issues.

Optimizing Perovskites with Machine Learning

  • Research Intern at the National University of Singapore's Solar Energy Research Institute of Singapore (SERIS) during the summer of 2023.
  • Project involved optimizing perovskite solar cells using machine learning techniques.
  • Implemented various deep learning models, including artificial neural networks (ANNs), random forests (RF), XGBoost, and stacked models, achieving up to 99.3% accuracy in predicting solar cell performance.
  • Conducted SHAP analysis to identify the most critical solar cell attributes.
  • Research was funded by the Engineering Science Research Opportunities Program (ESROP) and supervised by Prof. Erik Birgersson.
